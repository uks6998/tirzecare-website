What is it?

Understanding Tirzepatide

A breakthrough dual-action medication changing the landscape of weight management and type 2 diabetes treatment.

Clinical Results

In landmark trials, Tirzepatide delivered unprecedented weight loss results compared to other medications.

22.5%Avg. body weight lost
72%Patients lost 15%+ weight

Tirzepatide is a once-weekly injectable medication that works as a dual GIP and GLP-1 receptor agonist. Unlike older medications, it targets two key hunger and insulin hormones simultaneously.

Sold under brand names Mounjaro (for diabetes) and Tirzatrim (for weight loss), it has received FDA approval and is transforming patient outcomes worldwide.

  • Reduces appetite and food cravings significantly
  • Slows stomach emptying for longer fullness
  • Improves blood sugar control in type 2 diabetes
  • Weekly injection — easy to follow routine
  • Available in multiple doses (2.5mg to 15mg)
  • Works best combined with a healthy diet & exercise

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions patients ask most about Tirzepatide.

How long does it take for Tirzepatide to work? ▼
Most patients notice appetite reduction within the first 1–2 weeks. Significant weight loss (5–10%) typically becomes visible by weeks 8–12. Maximum results are usually seen around 72 weeks at the highest dose.
What BMI do I need to qualify for Tirzepatide? ▼
For weight loss (Tirzatrim), you generally need a BMI of 30+ (obese), or 27+ (overweight) with at least one weight-related condition such as high blood pressure, type 2 diabetes, or high cholesterol. Your doctor makes the final decision.
What are the most common side effects? ▼
The most common side effects are gastrointestinal: nausea, diarrhea, vomiting, and constipation. These are usually worst when starting or increasing the dose and tend to improve over time. Eating smaller, low-fat meals helps manage them.
Can I eat normally while on Tirzepatide? ▼
Tirzepatide works best with a reduced-calorie, balanced diet. Because the medication slows digestion, large fatty meals may worsen nausea. Focus on lean proteins, vegetables, whole grains, and small frequent meals for best results.
Is Tirzepatide available in Pakistan? ▼
Tirzepatide availability varies. As of 2025, it is primarily available in the US, EU, and select other markets. In Pakistan, some specialty clinics and pharmaceutical distributors may carry it. Consult your local physician or pharmacist for current availability.
Will I regain weight if I stop taking it? ▼
Studies show that stopping Tirzepatide can lead to weight regain, similar to other GLP-1 medications. This is why it's considered a long-term treatment. Lifestyle changes — diet and exercise — are crucial to maintain results if you discontinue.
